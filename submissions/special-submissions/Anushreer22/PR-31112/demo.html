Mission Profile

From Launch to Stratosphere

Each platform climbs through five distinct atmospheric layers before reaching stable cruise altitude.

0 – 2,000 ft

Ground Launch & Initial Climb

Electric motors engage at full thrust for vertical ascent through low-altitude turbulence and local air traffic corridors.

2,000 – 18,000 ft

Troposphere Transit

Solar arrays begin contributing meaningful charge as the drone climbs above cloud cover into clearer air.

18,000 – 35,000 ft

Jet Stream Crossing

Adaptive flight control compensates for high-speed jet stream winds while maintaining a stable ascent vector.

35,000 – 55,000 ft

Lower Stratosphere Entry

Air density drops sharply — control surfaces shift to a low-drag profile optimised for thin atmosphere.

55,000 – 65,000 ft

Stable Stratospheric Cruise

Drone reaches station-keeping altitude, solar output stabilises, and continuous atmospheric sensing begins.
